Application control is one of the most important control planes in a modern endpoint strategy, but it is also one of the easiest to implement badly.

## Why organisations get stuck

Many estates inherit third-party control products that have grown complex over time. They may solve immediate risk, but often at the cost of policy sprawl, exception handling overhead, and operational fragility.

## Better transition approach

A stronger model is to treat App Control for Business as a staged policy lifecycle:

1. Audit current execution patterns
2. Identify trusted publishers and managed paths
3. Build policy sets that reflect real application usage
4. Enforce in controlled rings
5. Establish an operational process for change

## Why this matters

When paired with Endpoint Privilege Management, application control becomes part of a broader trust model. Users operate with less standing privilege, execution is governed more explicitly, and support teams have a cleaner route for controlled elevation and exceptions.

The key is not speed. The key is policy maturity.
